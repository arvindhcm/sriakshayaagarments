About Us

Why India

Since 1990’s the business environment of india has changed the economy is exposed to a greater degree of external competition. More liberal trade and investment policies and improvement in the balance of payments and foreign exchange positions. The liberalisation has stimulated a wave of modernisation and development within the country’s clothing industry too.The textile and garments industry currently accounts for about 20% of india’s total exports.

India is a Major Sourcing hub for ready made garments for global companies. Lot of Major local exports players are located in tirupur.If needThe Apparel sectoris expected to expand even further following the expiration of the global textile and apparel industry. India expects to Capitalize on its Strong Multi-fibre base, large production of cotton, Low cost man power and presence across the entire value chain of textile and apparel industry.It is also has focus on value added products the challenge is to consolidate the industry. Which will result in economics of scale and resource becoming available for capital investment with which to upgrade plants and Machinery.

Why Tirupur

Tirupur is an important trade centre of india which is famous for knitted garments wears. Itis famous for the export of all Knitted garments wears such as T-Shirts , Polo Shirts, Sweat Shirts, Baniyans, Pyjamas and night dresses on Various fabrics like jersey, Interlock, fleece polyester pique, cotton, honey combed, baby pique, shining feather touch etc.Only Tirupur Seperately accounts for 56% India’s Cotton knitwear export. North an estimated Rs. 26000 cores. Tirupur has gained universal recognition as the leading source of Hosiery, Knitted garments, Casual Wears and Sport wear. The city accounts for Major India’s Cotton Knitwear export worth an estimated us $3bn.

The dramatic transformation of tirupur from domestic market to international market is Mainly attributed to the fact That most of western countries started disbanding their garments Manufacturing due to natural source, pollution and high cost of labour concurrently the Asian tigers Japan, Hong Kong, Taiwan and South Korea Started moving to hi-tech areas thus enabling Tirupur to capitalize the opportunity of penetrating in to the European Market.
